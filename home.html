Nepal Must Act on Climate Change

Nepal is among the most climate-vulnerable nations on Earth. Discover practical actions you, your community, and your campus can take right now to protect the Himalayas and the people who depend on them.

Why Climate Action Matters for Nepal

SDG 13 calls on all of us — as students, citizens and future professionals — to act urgently. For Nepal, a country sandwiched between two of the world's largest emitters, the stakes could not be higher.

Nepal's Crisis Is Now

Nepal has lost over 24% of its glacier ice since the 1970s. Glacial lake outburst floods (GLOFs), erratic monsoons and devastating landslides are intensifying year after year across the country.

  • Over 3,600 glacial lakes — many dangerously expanding
  • Millions at risk from floods in the Terai lowlands
  • Sacred Himalayan ecosystems rapidly disappearing

Nepali Students Can Lead Change

As students in Nepal, your daily decisions matter. From campus energy habits to food choices and how you travel between cities, you have real power to shape a more sustainable future for this country.

  • Choose local, seasonal, and plant-rich foods
  • Use micro-buses, e-rickshaws, or cycle where possible
  • Advocate for green policies at Tribhuvan University and beyond

Imagine Nepal's Green Future

Nepal already generates over 99% of its electricity from hydropower. If bold climate actions are widely adopted, Nepal could become a net-zero, climate-resilient nation and a model for the Global South.

  • Carbon-neutral economy powered by hydro and solar
  • Thousands of green jobs in conservation and clean energy
  • Cleaner air in Kathmandu Valley and healthier mountain communities
